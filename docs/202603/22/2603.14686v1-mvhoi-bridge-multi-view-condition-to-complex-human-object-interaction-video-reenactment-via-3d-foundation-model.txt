## Abstract 

Human-Object Interaction (HOI) video reenactment with realistic motion remains a frontier in expressive digital human creation. Existing approaches primarily handle simple image-plane motion (e.g . in-plane translations), struggling with complex non-planar manipulations like out-of-plane reorientation. In this paper, we propose MVHOI, a two-stage HOI video reenactment framework that bridges multi-view reference conditions and video foundation models via a 3D Foundation Model (3DFM). ## 1 Introduction 

The field of controllable human video generation [ 4 , 8, 11 , 22 ] has a rapid evolution, empowering a diverse array of applications by enabling sophisticated motion manipulation and significantly en-hancing the user experience. As the fidelity of digital human an-imation reaches new heights, there is an escalating demand for more expressive synthesis capabilities, particularly in the realm of Human-Object Interaction (HOI). Benefiting from the formidable generative power of Video Foun-dation Models [ 16 , 28 , 39 ] (VFMs), HOI scenarios can theoretically be synthesized through detailed prompt descriptions. However, this process remains largely non-deterministic and fails to consistently yield stable or satisfactory results for precise manipulation tasks. While some derivative works [ 12 , 15 , 20 , 44 ] attempt to incorporate multiple reference images to achieve prompt-following generation, they frequently suffer from significant losses in size and appearance consistency. Such physically disharmonized artifacts and deforma-tions limit their applicability in demanding real-world scenarios. Recently, an emerging body of works [ 7, 13 , 30 , 36 ] has pivoted toward HOI reenactment within human videos, which usually em-ploy a combination of sparse 2D descriptors(e.g., bounding box or keypoint) and monocular reference images for representing objects. While effective for simple 2D translational movements, this paradigm falters when confronted with complex 3D dynamics, such as rapid object rotations. We posit that the failure of exist-ing methods in these scenarios stems from two fundamental, yet unresolved challenges: 1) Representation Gap in Complex 3D Dynamics : Existing 2D-based descriptors suffer from an inherent structural gap when navigating complex non-planar dynamics . Due to a lack of spatial granularity, these sparse proxies cannot capture the view-contingent geometric deformations essential for realistic HOI. While one might consider explicit 6D pose estimation as a remedy, such strategies are notoriously prone to orientation am-biguity, especially when encountering symmetric or texture-less objects. These uncertainties result in discontinuous pose trajecto-ries and physically implausible artifacts, causing the synthesized object to “drift” or de-synchronize from the intricate articulation of the human hand. 2) Ill-posed Texture Synthesis and View Inconsistency: HOI rotation necessitates the synthesis of consis-tent appearance across diverse viewpoints. However, relying on a monocular reference image provides insufficient visual cues for the occluded or rotated surfaces, forcing the video generation models into stochastic generation which inevitably results in multi-view inconsistency. Even when multiple reference views are available, the effective fusion of these textures—especially in the presence of dynamic hand-object occlusions—remains an open and non-trivial problem. To address these challenges, we introduce MVHOI, a two-stage framework that leverages the spatial priors of 3D Foundation Mod-els (3DFMs) to redefine HOI video reenactment. Our core insight is that 3DFMs provide a decisive advantage: they can consolidate sparse multi-view inputs into a Unified Object Anchor within the la-tent space, a view-invariant repository where geometric structure and visual appearance are inherently aligned. Under this paradigm, the challenge of HOI reenactment is transformed from a stochastic gen-eration task into a deterministic view-querying process, enabling the precise retrieval of projections from this persistent 3D anchor. Specifically, the first stage of our framework focuses on reconcil-ing the complex 3D dynamics of the source interaction. Rather than relying on error-prone explicit pose estimation, we propose a 3D-aware Object Reenactment Module built upon this implicit Unified Object Anchor. By extracting motion latents from the source video, we establish a continuous trajectory directly within the 3DFM’s latent space. This allows the model to “navigate” the Unified Object Anchor and query the corresponding viewpoints with unprece-dented geometric robustness, yielding a coarse but geometrically-consistent reenacted result of the target object. By bypassing unre-liable offline trackers, this “Object-to-Object” mechanism ensures that the generated motion remains physically plausible and syn-chronized with the human hand throughout the interaction. In the second stage, we transition from geometric alignment to high-fidelity video synthesis. To maintain appearance consistency under complex motions, it is essential to effectively integrate visual cues from multi-view reference images of the target object. How-ever, simply providing these references to a video diffusion model often leads to texture mismatch, as the model struggles to identify which viewpoint to consult for a specific frame. To resolve this, we discover that the internal mechanisms of the 3DFM, specifically the attention biases that emerged during Stage-I implicit motion modeling, serve as a powerful proxy for spatial correspondence. We novelly design a multi-view appearance retrieval mechanism based on these intermediate cues to steer the video diffusion process. Unlike standard generation that may stochastically “hallucinate” occluded surfaces, our retrieval mechanism provides the diffusion model with a similarity reference enabling it to precisely retrieve vi-sual features from the correct multi-view references. This effectively eliminates semantic drifting and viewpoint confusion, ensuring that even during rapid 360 ◦ rotations or complex hand-object occlusions, the synthesized interactions remain photorealistic and temporally coherent. Our contributions are summarized below: 1) We introduce the first HOI video reenactment framework capable of synthesizing complex non-planar dynamics, effectively transcending the limita-tions of 2D translational models to achieve realistic object swapping under agile 3D transformations. 2) We propose a novel 3D-aware Object Reenactment Module built upon 3D Foundation Models that enables view-dependent reenactment via querying its implicit unified object anchor. 3) We propose a multi-view texture synthe-sis scheme and a corresponding retrieval mechanism to recapture relevant visual features for distinct viewpoints. 

## 2 Related Work 2.1 Controllable Video Generation and Editing 

Video generation models have advanced rapidly in recent years. Early methods [ 1, 9, 33 ] adapted image diffusion models [ 27 ] to video, while more recent transformer-based models [ 2, 16 , 28 , 39 ]improve temporal coherence, motion realism, and visual fidelity MVHOI Conference acronym ’XX, June 03–05, 2018, Woodstock, NY 

through larger-scale training. Several controllable editing meth-ods extend these backbones with reference images or conditioning branches to preserve subject or object appearance during genera-tion [ 12 , 15 , 20 ]. These models are strong general-purpose editors, but they do not explicitly model the 3D object state required for HOI reenactment under large viewpoint changes. In particular, complex object rotation and hand-object occlusion still lead to viewpoint confusion and appearance drift when conditioning remains primar-ily 2D. 

## 2.2 Human-Object Interaction 

Recent HOI synthesis methods adapt diffusion or video-generation pipelines to model hand-object interactions more explicitly. DiffHOI [ 40 ] models the conditional distribution of object renderings for HOI synthesis, while HOI-Swap [ 35 ] extends static HOI inpainting to videos through sequential warping. OpenHOI [ 43 ] combines affordance grounding, multimodal reasoning, and diffusion-based generation, and HunyuanVideo-HOMA [ 13 ] reduces reliance on heavily curated motion data by using a weakly conditioned multi-modal diffusion transformer. ReHold [ 7 ] and related reenactment pipelines [ 30 , 36 ] move closer to the setting studied here, but they still depend mainly on 2D control signals or limited object refer-ences. As a result, they remain vulnerable to non-planar motion, view-dependent appearance change, and hand-object occlusion. Our work addresses this setting by coupling multi-view object references with an explicit 3D-aware latent representation before video synthesis. 

## 2.3 3D Foundation Models 

The emergence of 3D foundation models marks a shift toward large-scale feed-forward backbones that learn transferable geometric representations across views. These models are increasingly used as plug-and-play priors for downstream 3D tasks rather than as task-specific reconstruction systems. Depth Anything [ 37 ] and Depth Anything V2 [ 38 ] show that large-scale training can yield robust depth estimation across diverse scenes. DUSt3R [ 31 ] reframes unconstrained reconstruction as feed-forward dense 3D prediction from image pairs, while VGGT [ 29 ]and PI3 [ 32 ] extend this trend toward broader multi-view prediction of geometric attributes. More recently, Depth Anything 3 [ 17 ] aims to recover a shared visual space from arbitrary input views, making it particularly relevant for downstream tasks that need consistent geometry across viewpoint changes. Our method uses this family of models as a geometric prior for HOI reenactment: rather than reconstructing a scene explicitly, we exploit a 3D foundation model to build a view-consistent latent anchor for target-object motion transfer and appearance retrieval.

## 3.3 Multi-reference Video Generation 

Building on the coarse guidance { ˆO𝑖 ·Δ𝑡 }𝑇 // Δ𝑡 𝑖 =0 from Stage I, we de-velop a DiT-based video inpainting model to recover high-frequency texture details and produce temporal conherence HOI videos. We observe that the coarse reenactment sequence primarily provides (i) pose and orientation consistent with the object motion in the driving video, and (ii) coarse appearance cues. While such guidance is essential for ensuring that the generated object follows the target motion, it is insufficient for generating sharp object textures and fine-grained details. To this end, we additionally use high-quality multi-view reference images I𝑟𝑒 𝑓 of the object in the video genera-tion model. 

Multi-reference adapter. To inject these conditioning signals into the DiT-based video generation backbone [ 28 ], we adopt an addi-tional context adapter [ 15 ] design by cloning specific intemediate blocks of the backbone. We prepend the high-quality multi-view reference images I𝑟𝑒 𝑓 at the beginning of the input sequence and input to a cloned context branch, preserving their high resolution and photometric fidelity; and incorporate the time-aligned coarse guidance into the masked video stream using the HOI mask. We leverage self-attention to enable the coarse guidance to steer the model toward the most relevant reference views: intuitively, the guidance frames indicate which reference view(s) best match the current object orientation, encouraging the network to retrieve detailed textures and view-specific cues from the corresponding references. Training the video generation model is not straightforward be-cause it relies on the coarse reenactment sequence which is pro-duced by the UOA module. End-to-end training would significantly increase computational and wall-clock cost, which is prohibitive for training such a video generation model. ## 3.5 Cross-iterative long-video inference. 

A central challenge in long video generation is preserving the sta-bility and appearance consistency of the interacting object over extended durations, including its shape, texture, and appearance consistency, while maintaining temporally stable interaction dy-namics. Naive auto-regressive inference strategies typically roll out the video continuously from previously generated frames, which of-ten suffers from error accumulation, leading to gradual appearance drift in the manipulated object, such as degraded texture quality, color inconsistency, and loss of shape fidelity. To address this issue, we propose a cross-iterative long-video inference strategy that alternates between coarse anchor-view gen-eration and high-quality video refinement. Specifically, for each temporal segment, our UOA module first predicts a sparse set of an-chor views that serve as coarse geometric and motion guidance over a relatively long horizon. These anchor views capture the overall interaction structure and object motion, but may lack fine-grained appearance details. We then feed them into the video generation model, which reconstructs a temporally coherent video clip with high-quality texture, appearance, and motion details. Importantly, instead of directly continuing auto-regressive roll-out from low-quality intermediate predictions, we use the seg-mented object from the final frame of the refined video clip as the initialization for the next temporal segment. In this way, the high-fidelity output of the video generator provides a stronger vi-sual prior for the subsequent round of coarse guidance generation, while the newly predicted anchor views in turn constrain the next video segment at the motion level. This alternating process forms a cross-iterative inference loop between anchor-view planning and video refinement, effectively suppressing cumulative drift and sub-stantially improving long-range identity consistency, appearance fidelity, and temporal stability in HOI video generation.

Evaluation dataset. We curated multiple datasets to ensure a com-prehensive evaluation. First, we utilized the AnchorCrafter dataset [34 ], specifically selecting 29 representative samples from the train-ing set to evaluate performance on the Cross-Reenactment task. Given that AnchorCrafter provides only three distinct viewpoints per object, our evaluation on this dataset is constrained to these three views. In addition to public benchmarks, we evaluated our method on 100 videos drawn from a self-collected dataset. 

Evaluation setting. We evaluate our method under two settings 

Self-Reenactment and Cross-Reenactment and assess perfor-mance from multiple aspects, including appearance quality, tempo-ral consistency, and object fidelity and user study. Please see the Appendix for more details on the evaluation metrics. 

Self-Reenactment. We condition on a masked driving video and regenerate the original sequence while preserving the same ob-ject identity. The goal is to faithfully reconstruct the input video, matching the pose, motion, and temporal dynamics of the driving sequence. 

Cross-Reenactment. We condition on a masked driving video but replace the original object with a different target object. The goal is to synthesize a new sequence that preserves the driving motion trajectory while transferring the target object’s appearance, i.e., changing object identity without altering the underlying interaction dynamics.

MimicMotion is pose-guided human video generation; VACE and HunyuanCustom are unified video generation/editing models; and HuMo supports multi-subject generation conditioned on reference subjects and text prompts.

## 4.4 Ablation Study 

We conduct an ablation study to evaluate the contribution of two key components in our framework: coarse reenactment guidance (CRG) and the inference-time attention enhancement . As the baseline, we train a multi-reference adapter conditioned solely on multi-view reference images. Quantitative results are reported in Tab. 4, with qualitative comparisons shown in Fig. 6. Overall, both components contribute consistently to improved generation quality and temporal stability. Adding CRG yields per-formance gain by providing explicit motion-aware structural guid-ance, which better anchors object dynamics and reduces temporal inconsistency. Building on this, the inference-time attention en-hancement further improves appearance fidelity by encouraging the model to retrieve viewpoint-consistent details from the multi-view references, thereby alleviating view confusion and enhancing cross-frame appearance consistency. These effects are clearly re-flected in both the quantitative results and the qualitative examples in Fig. 6.

We present MVHOI , a novel framework for 3D-aware human-object interaction video generation that bridges multi-view object condi-tions and HOI video reenactment through 3D foundation models. MVHOI Conference acronym ’XX, June 03–05, 2018, Woodstock, NY 

Our method addresses a core challenge in object-centric video gen-eration: preserving object identity, geometric consistency, and inter-action realism under large pose changes, severe occlusions, and com-plex hand-object dynamics. To this end, we introduce a 3D-aware object reenactment module that uses a unified object anchor to pro-vide motion-aware structural guidance, together with a multi-view texture synthesis and retrieval mechanism for viewpoint-consistent appearance generation. Extensive experiments demonstrate that MVHOI achddieves superior performance across object reenactment, HOI video gener-ation, and long-horizon synthesis settings, consistently improving both quantitative metrics and perceptual quality. These results ver-ify that incorporating explicit 3D-aware guidance and multi-view appearance modeling is an effective direction for controllable and realistic HOI video generation.
