# MAPRPose: Mask-Aware Proposal and Amodal Refinement for Multi-Object 6D Pose Estimation 

Yang Luo , Yan Gong , Yongsheng Gao* , Xiaoying Sun* , Jie Zhao , Senior Member, IEEE 

Abstract —6D object pose estimation in cluttered scenes remains challenging due to severe occlusion and sensor noise. We propose MAPRPose, a two-stage framework that leverages mask-aware correspondences for pose proposal and amodal-driven Region-of-Interest (ROI) prediction for robust refinement. In the Mask-Aware Pose Proposal (MAPP) stage, we lift 2D correspondences into 3D space to establish reliable keypoint matches and generate geometrically consistent pose hypotheses based on correspondence-level scoring, from which the top-K candidates are selected. In the refinement stage, we introduce a tensorized render-and-compare pipeline integrated with an Amodal Mask Prediction and ROI Re-Alignment (AMPR) module. By reconstructing complete object geometry and dynamically adjusting the ROI, AMPR mitigates localization errors and spatial misalignment under heavy occlusion. Furthermore, our GPU-accelerated RGB-XYZ reprojection enables simultaneous refinement of all N × B pose hypotheses in a single forward pass. Evaluated on the BOP benchmark, MAPRPose achieves a state-of-the-art Average Recall (AR) of 76.5%, outperforming FoundationPose by 3.1% AR while delivering a 43 × speedup in multi-object inference. 

Index Terms —6D pose estimation, occlusion handling, mask-aware proposal, amodal mask prediction 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

6D object pose estimation, which recovers the 3D rota-tion and translation of objects from visual observations, is fundamental for autonomous robotic systems [1]–[17]. A particularly important yet challenging scenario is zero-shot pose estimation of novel objects, given their CAD models, under heavy occlusion and background clutter with strict requirements on computational efficiency. This capability is critical for applications such as conveyor-belt manipulation and large-scale warehouse bin-picking in unstructured environments [18]. Although supervised methods excel at instance-level estimation for known objects, real-world deployment requires robust generalization to novel instances. This challenge is further compounded in cluttered environments with partial observa-tions. Moreover, practical systems demand high computational efficiency for real-world deployment.

To mitigate the adverse effects of occlusion and background clutter, recent studies have increasingly incorporated mask-based strategies into the pose estimation pipeline. For instance, methods such as PoseCNN [19] and One2Any [20] employ instance-level masks as spatial priors, whereas multi-task architectures like SO-Pose [21] and Self6D [22] integrate amodal segmentation to recover complete object geometry. Despite these advancements, such approaches still suffer from two critical limitations. First, their heavy reliance on object-centric cropping frequently yields suboptimal initial translation estimates under severe occlusion. Second, this rigid, sequential optimization process makes them highly susceptible to local optima, often requiring dozens of iterations to achieve high pose accuracy, which severely limits their scalability in complex, multi-object scenes. To overcome the susceptibility to local optima, recent works [23]–[25] have shifted toward the render-and-compare paradigm. By iteratively rendering pose hypotheses and com-paring them against the query image, methods such as Mega-Pose [23] and FoundationPose [24] achieve strong robustness and zero-shot generalization. However, this paradigm faces a fundamental trade-off between hypothesis coverage and computational efficiency in complex, occluded, and multi-object scenes. As illustrated in Fig. 1, we depart from baseline frame-works [25], [26] that rely on unconstrained feature matching. Instead, we formulate the 6D pose estimation as a partial-to-amodal geometric alignment problem. We present MAPRPose, a unified framework that synergizes mask-aware proposal gen-eration and amodal-driven refinement to address the decoupled components of 6D pose initialization and optimization. In the pose proposal stage, we introduce Mask-Aware Pose Proposal (MAPP) to provide rotation initialization. By constraining correspondence search within the visible object manifold, MAPP neutralizes background clutter and prunes the search space from hundreds of templates to a compact set of Top-

K hypotheses. This not only avoids the local optima caused by the initialization drift but also reduces computational cost. To alleviate initialization bias in translation estimation, we introduce the Amodal Mask Prediction and ROI Re-Alignment (AMPR) mechanism. While conventional pipelines frequently suffer from suboptimal translation estimates due to cropping biased towards visible regions, AMPR reconstructs the full object geometry to calibrate the ROI’s center and scale prior to refinement. This ensures that the subsequent optimization is anchored to the true object centroid, effectively rectifying the localization errors inherent in partial observations. Crucially, our framework re-architects the render-and-compare paradigm into a fully tensorized refinement and re-projection pipeline. Conventional approaches typically process hypotheses and objects sequentially. This leads to low GPU utilization when the hypothesis space is small. In contrast, we integrate the refinement network and the RGB-XYZ re-projection process into a unified tensorized operator. This enables the simultaneous optimization of all N × B pose hypotheses ( N objects, B

hypotheses) in a single forward pass. By reclaiming the GPU throughput that would otherwise be wasted on individual hypothesis processing, our pipeline alleviates the linear scaling bottleneck, achieving a 43 × throughput speedup over Foun-dationPose [24] while maintaining a 76.5% AR on the BOP benchmark. A. CAD Model-based Methods 

CAD-model-based methods are the predominant approach for zero-shot 6D pose estimation of novel objects. These methods can be broadly categorized into correspondence-based methods and render & compare methods, with many recent works [23], [25]–[27] utilizing hybrid two-stage pipelines that combine fast proposal generation with iterative refinement. Correspondence-based methods, such as DenseFusion [28], DPOD [29], Sur-fEmb [30], GeoPose [31] and others [32]–[35], establish dense 2D-3D correspondences or establish matching relationships via iterative feature fusion between query observations and CAD-derived representations. These established correspondences are then processed by geometric solvers (e.g., PnP [36] or RANSAC [37]) for final pose estimation. Although these methods are computationally efficient, they remain vulnerable to segmentation inaccuracies. In particular, severe occlusions often lead to a significant initialization drift in pose estimation. Moreover, the lack of discriminative local geometric cues makes it difficult to distinguish foreground from background, resulting in erroneous correspondences and preventing accurate 6D pose recovery from partially visible observations. Render & compare methods, including MegaPose [23], Foun-dationPose [24], SAM-6D [38], FreeZe [39], FreeZeV2 [27], and Geo6D [40], evaluate multiple pose hypotheses by reducing the visual or geometric discrepancy between the rendered model and the observed query. Although these frameworks are generally more robust to occlusion and environmental noise than pure correspondence-based techniques, they often incur higher computational overhead due to the iterative nature of the alignment process. To further enhance this paradigm, specialized approaches have been developed for complex scenarios: EPOS [41] introduces effective handling of both discrete and continuous symmetries, while CosyPose [42] enforces global geometric consistency by optimizing 6D poses across multiple views. Despite their differences, these paradigms share two critical limitations. First, hybrid pipelines that combine both strategies 3

(e.g., Co-op [26], GigaPose [25], FreeZeV2 [27]) frequently suffer from dense correspondence matching [25], [26] that is prone to corruption by cluttered backgrounds and severe occlusion. This lack of explicit geometric grounding during feature matching leads to decoupled reasoning, making it difficult to achieve robust pose estimation. Second, the compu-tational scalability of these methods is strictly limited by the heavy per-object rendering overhead (e.g., nvdiffrast [43]), which hinders efficient parallel computing in multi-object scenes. These challenges highlight the need for approaches that integrate mask-aware correspondence modeling with scalable geometric reasoning across both proposal and refinement stages, ensuring that optimization remains focused on the object’s visible manifold. 

B. Occlusion-Aware Pose Estimation 

Occlusion-aware pose estimation has evolved from correspondence-based methods [44]–[47] to mask-guided frameworks like [21], [48], [49], which leverage visible masks as spatial priors, but remain sensitive to incomplete observations. However, a fundamental bottleneck persists: the systemic spatial-scale misalignment inherent in traditional, visible-mask-driven Region of Interest (ROI) extraction. Ex-isting pipelines typically operate in an open-loop manner, where cropping based solely on visible fragments shifts the image-plane localization ( tx, t y ) and introduces critical scale-inconsistency during resizing. This artificial magnification distorts the perceived object scale relative to its canonical CAD reference, inevitably corrupting depth ( tz ) inference. To address these gaps, recent trends have shifted to amodal-aware geometric recovery. For instance, UA-Pose [50] intro-duces uncertainty-aware completion to handle partial references, while other works [51], [52] explore amodal completion and open-vocabulary reasoning to resolve pose ambiguities. Building upon this paradigm, we propose a multi-hypothesis framework that replaces the conventional one-pass cropping with a recursive feedback mechanism. By dynamically re-aligning the ROI based on predicted full-object geometry, our approach maintains global geometric consistency and rectifies the scale-inconsistency that plagues previous occlusion-aware pipelines. III. M ETHODOLOGY 

A. Overview 

We now present MAPRPose, a two-stage framework that combines mask-aware pose proposal with amodal-driven refine-ment (Fig. 3). In the MAPP stage, correspondences between the target query and reference templates are extracted using DINOv2 [53] features and lifted to 3D to generate top-K

geometrically consistent pose hypotheses. These proposals are then passed to the Pose Refinement stage, which incorporates an AMPR module to rectify location and scale discrepancies for precise spatial consistency. Furthermore, the refinement stage predicts relative pose increments and performs fast RGB-XYZ re-projection to synthesize pixel-aligned reference-to-query representations.

A. Benchmark Datasets and Evaluation Metrics 

We evaluate our proposed method on the LINEMOD dataset [65] and the BOP benchmark datasets [66] (including LM-O, T-LESS, TUD-L, IC-BIN, ITODD, HB, and YCB-V). These datasets cover a wide range of challenging scenarios (e.g., severe occlusion, dense clutter, and single- and multi-object settings) and include objects with diverse properties (e.g., texture-less surfaces, reflective materials, symmetries, and varying scales). The LINEMOD dataset mainly focuses on instance-level 6D pose estimation of texture-rich objects under relatively controlled environments, and is widely used for evaluating accuracy in single-object settings. In contrast, the BOP benchmark comprises multiple datasets collected from real-world and industrial scenarios, increasing the complexity of the task. It includes challenging cases such as heavy occlusion (LM-O), texture-less objects with strong symmetries (T-LESS), and diverse household objects with varying shapes and ap-pearances (YCB-V). B. Experimental Setup 

We conducted three sets of experiments to evaluate the accuracy and efficiency of our method across various scenarios. On the LINEMOD dataset, we utilize ground-truth masks to assess the performance of our refinement module under the ADD-0.1d metric, reporting both accuracy and single-object runtime latency on an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4090 GPU. For the more challenging BOP Challenge benchmarks, including LM-O, YCB-V, and T-LESS, evaluations are performed on an NVIDIA A100 GPU using object masks pre-estimated by SAM-6D [38]. To maintain a consistent computational flow as described in Section III, we adopt a tensorized batching strategy for all refinement experiments. By setting N = 5 and B = 7, our framework processes N × B = 35 hypothesis-reference pairs in a single unified forward pass. This parallel configuration, executed over i = 4 iterations, allows MAPRPose to achieve over 90% GPU utilization on an NVIDIA A100 GPU. In contrast, FoundationPose processes only one hypothesis at a time and under-utilizes the GPU. Our evaluation protocols focus on both accuracy and system throughput. For the BOP benchmark, we evaluate the Average Recall (AR) and record the total processing time per image. This metric accounts for the simultaneous handling of multiple object instances within a single scene, providing a realistic measure of multi-object inference speed. Additionally, we con-ducted ablation studies to systematically examine the influence of key components on both accuracy and speed, including the number of refinement iterations, the top-K (K = 7 ) parameter for hypothesis selection, and the contribution of the amodal mask prediction module.

D. Ablation Studies 

While our method achieves a balance between precision and efficiency across multiple benchmarks, this section dissects the specific contributions of our key components. We evaluate the mask-aware proposal module on LINEMOD and the amodal mask prediction module on the LM-O and YCB-V datasets to analyze their robustness against occlusion and clutter. 

(1) Analysis of the MAPP Module: To evaluate the effec-tiveness of the proposed MAPP module, we compare a hybrid variant, MAPP+FP (where MAPP generates coarse proposals for FoundationPose [24]), with the original FoundationPose pipeline. As shown in Table III, MAPP+FP achieves an ADD-0.1d accuracy of 99.4%, only 0.5% lower than the full FoundationPose (99.9%) that relies on 252 hypotheses. This marginal drop indicates that the constrained search space preserves the high-quality hypotheses required for downstream refinement, while significantly improving sampling efficiency. Furthermore, MAPRPose (w/o MAPP) reduces the number of candidate poses from 252 to 7 without any loss in accuracy (99.9%), demonstrating the effectiveness of our constrained pose sampling strategy in eliminating physically implausible viewpoints. The choice of Top-K = 7 is motivated by the geometric redundancy of the SO (3) search space. Dense sampling strategies, such as those used in FoundationPose, often generate redundant candidates corresponding to equivalent viewpoints, particularly for symmetric objects. As shown in Table III (Part B), increasing Top-K from 3 to 7 improves accuracy from 97.2% to 99.7%, with notable gains on ambiguous objects such as cat and can . However, further increasing Top-K yields diminishing returns, with only a marginal improvement to 99.8% at K = 11 .These results suggest that K = 7 is sufficient to capture the multi-modal pose distribution, including symmetry-induced ambiguities. Compared to the 252-hypothesis baseline, our method reduces the computational cost by approximately 97%, while achieving stable recall across both symmetric and asymmetric objects with a low inference latency of 0.05s.

E. Runtime Analysis 

We evaluate the computational efficiency of MAPRPose from two perspectives: single-object latency and system-level throughput. As shown in Table III, our method achieves a single-object inference latency of 0.05 s on an NVIDIA RTX 4090, represent-ing a 24 × speedup over the 1.2 s required by FoundationPose. This significant latency reduction is primarily attributed to two key architectural optimizations: the MAPP module prunes the initial sampling space down to only B = 7 high-quality hypotheses, and the amodal mask prediction in AMPR provides geometrically consistent initialization that substantially reduces the number of refinement iterations i required for convergence. As shown in Table VI, our design exhibits superior scalability in multi-object scenarios. By replacing sequential rendering with a tensorized execution model, MAPRPose achieves 1.47 FPS for full-scene inference ( N = 5 ), a 43 × throughput improvement over FoundationPose (1.47 FPS vs. 0.034 FPS per frame). Notably, as N increases, our framework can saturate the GPU, elevating utilization from 70% to over 90% and further boosting the inference steady-state to 1.50 FPS. These results demonstrate that the N × B batching strategy successfully achieves high-throughput pose estimation without compromising accuracy, effectively utilizing modern hardware parallelism. V. C ONCLUSION 

We present MAPRPose, a hierarchical 6D pose estimation framework designed to harness visible masks as explicit geometric priors, mitigating the impact of severe occlu-sion and background clutter in zero-shot scenarios. The MAPP module systematically prunes the hypothesis space via mask-constrained correspondences. Simultaneously, the AMPR mechanism rectifies truncation effects through joint amodal prediction and dynamic ROI normalization, bolstering robustness against partial visibility. By transcending conven-tional CAD-based rendering in favor of a tensorized RGB-XYZ re-projection scheme, our pipeline facilitates massive parallelization across multiple instances and hypotheses. This architecture achieves a 43× increase in inference throughput over FoundationPose while maintaining competitive precision.
