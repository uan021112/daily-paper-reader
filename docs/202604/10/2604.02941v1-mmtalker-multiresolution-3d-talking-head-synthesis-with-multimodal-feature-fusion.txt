# MMTalker: Multiresolution 3D Talking Head Synthesis with Multimodal Feature Fusion 

Bin Liu,Zhixiang Xiong,Zhifen He,Bo Li 

Abstract —Speech-driven three-dimensional (3D) facial anima-tion synthesis aims to build a mapping from one-dimensional (1D) speech signals to time-varying 3D facial motion signals. Current methods still face challenges in maintaining lip-sync accuracy and producing realistic facial expressions, primarily due to the highly ill-posed nature of this cross-modal mapping. In this paper, we introduce a novel 3D audio-driven facial animation synthe-sis method through multi-resolution representation and multi-modal feature fusion, called MMTalker which can accurately reconstruct the rich details of 3D facial motion. We first achieve the continuous representation of 3D face with details by mesh parameterization and non-uniform differentiable sampling. The mesh parameterization technique establishes the correspondence between UV plane and 3D facial mesh and is used to offer ground truth for the continuous learning. Differentiable non-uniform sampling enables precise facial detail acquisition by setting learnable sampling probability in each triangular face. Next, we employ residual graph convolutional network and dual cross-attention mechanism to extract discriminative facial motion feature from multiple input modalities. This proposed multimodal fusion strategy takes full use of the hierarchical features of speech and the explicit spatiotemporal geometric features of facial mesh. Finally, a lightweight regression network predicts the vertex-wise geometric displacements of the synthesized talking face by jointly processing the sampled points in the canonical UV space and the encoded facial motion features. Comprehensive exper-iments demonstrate that significant improvements are achieved over state-of-the-art methods, especially in the synchronization accuracy of lip and eye movements. 

Index Terms —Audio-driven face animation, Multi-resolution representation, Multi-modal feature fusion, Differentiable sam-pling. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

# AS a cutting-edge research, facial animation synthesis integrates multiple application technologies such as arti-ficial intelligence, natural language processing and computer graphics. It can bring unprecedented experiences to fields such as virtual reality, personal assistants, film production and education. The speech-driven 3D facial animation synthesis method essentially realizes the mapping from 1D voice signals to 3D time-varying facial motion signals, and finally generates facial animations with consistent lip movements, realistic expressions and motions. The main difficulty is the highly ill-posed nature of this cross-modal mapping. The basic strategy of these parametric representation methods [3]–[6] is to learn the mapping from voice signals to facial parametric representation coefficients through data-driven technology, and finally reconstruct a 3D face through the parametric representation model. However, limited by the linearity assumption of the facial representation model, this kind of methods are difficult to represent facial expression details such as wrinkles. In order to regress more realistic facial geometry, researchers [7]–[10] introduce the animation synthesis by directly using the 3D facial mesh representation. The main idea of these methods is to use deep learning technology to directly regress the positions of vertices on the facial mesh or the displacements relative to the template in an end-to-end manner, which has good flexibility. However, the facial surface is essentially a two-dimensional manifold, which is non-convex in the three-dimensional space, resulting in this kind of method being easy to fall into local optimal solutions when optimizing the vertices or offsets of face mesh. In addition, limited by the fix representation of the face template or parametric representation, these methods can only generate facial animations with the same geometry structure, which are difficult for applications that require various face resolutions. • A continuous and detailed facial representation method is introduced based on mesh parameterization and non-uniform differentiable sampling, which reduces the opti-mization difficulty of facial mesh representation. 

• A new multi-modal fusion strategy is proposed based on geometry-guided graph convolutional network and dual cross-attention mechanism, which further reduce the uncertainty of cross-modal mapping for talking face animation.

III. M ULTI -RESOLUTION 3D T ALKING FACE SYNTHESIS 

In this chapter, we introduce the proposed multi-resolution 3D talking face synthesis with multi-modal feature fusion. Given a template facial mesh and an input audio, our goal is to generate a realistic and detailed 3D head animation at different resolutions with the input audio. The architecture of the proposed method is illustrated in Fig 2. We first use mesh parameterization technology [11] to obtain the UV planar representation of the template mesh and get the 3D face with different resolution by an detail-preserved non-uniform sampling strategy. Next, the spatio-temporal position coding features of mesh vertices, the local vertex-wise features of audio clip and the global feature of audio clip are fused through graph convolutional neural network and dual-channel cross-attention mechanism. B. Mesh Parameterization and Non-uniform Sampling 

Mesh parameterization is an important technique in com-puter graphics and geometric processing. Its core goal is to establish the mapping between a UV parameter domain and the three-dimensional mesh surface. To further maintain the invariance of scaling rates and local angle in the surface mesh, we adopt the conformal mapping [11]. Since UV parameter domain stores the same geometry topology as the template mesh I, we can easily build the continuous correspondence between them by barycentric interpolation. In addition, to make the generated face have more facial details, we perform non-uniform sampling in the UV parameter domain. For each triangle in the face mesh, its sampling probability is related to the distance from the key points on the human face. Figure 3 shows the performance of mesh parameterization and non-uniform sampling. 

C. Multi-modal Semantic Alignment 

In this section, we first introduce how to extract the spatio-temporal encoding features of mesh vertices, the local vertex-wise encoding features of audio and the global encoding feature of audio. Then, in order to achieve the alignment of audio and motion modalities, the multi-model feature fusion strategy from these above features will be displayed in detail. 

Spatio-temporal vertex feature encoding. In order to en-hance the alignment of cross-modal mapping, previous meth-ods [7]–[9] employ encoder-decoder structured networks to build the mapping between audio and facial space. VOCA [7] only consider the single speech modality in the encoder structure. Although GDPnet [8] and MeshTalk [9] take face mesh as part of the input in encoder structure, they use the global features of mesh while discarding the local features. Hence, this oversimplified form leads to ambiguous local correspondences. In addition, none of the aforementioned methods utilize local spatial positional encoding techniques, nor do they incorporate temporal sequence encoding. The only method employing temporal sequence encoding is Face-Former [31]. However, they integrate the temporal encoding with the global features of the facial mesh, which similarly JOURNAL OF L ATEX CLASS FILES, VOL. 14, NO. 8, AUGUST 2021 4

Local and global audio feature encoding. Following these methods [31], [32] we also use the architecture of the state-of-the-art self-supervised pre-trained speech model wav2vec2.0 [35] for audio feature extraction. The difference is that we extract global feature and local vertex-wise feature from audio. The global feature relates to facial expression which are crucial to facial movements. The local vertex-wise feature is able to capture subtle nuances of mouth and eye movements which are important for creating realistic facial animation. In our implementation, we get the final layer feature di from the pre-trained Wav2Vec2.0 model for each audio clip and interpolate the output with the desired frame rate to obtain the latent audio features. The global audio feature fag ∈ RT ×H are obtained by encoding the latent audio features using the long short-term memory network, where H denotes the feature dimension. The local vertex-wise audio feature fal ∈ RT ×N ×H are extracted by applying 1D convolution and multi-layer perceptrons to the latent audio features. 

Multi-modal feature fusion. EXPERIMENT TODO In this section, we first introduce the implementation details and dataset used in the proposed method. The effec-tiveness of this method was verified through qualitative and quantitative experiments. Finally, an ablation study experiment was conducted to verify the effectiveness of the proposed module. 

Implementation details: We train the network using the Adam optimization algorithm on NVIDIA RTX 3090 GPU,and set the learning rate and batch size to 1e-4 and 1. The values of the hyperparameters λ1, λ2, and λ3 in the loss function are specified as 1, 10, and 0.01. 

VOCASET Dataset: The VOCASET dataset [2] includes a comprehensive collection of audio-4D scan pairs obtained from 6 female and 6 male subjects, which contains 480 facial motion sequences from 12 subjects, captured at 60fps for approximately 4 seconds per sequence.Each 3D face mesh is registered to the FLAME [2] topology with 5023 vertices. 

Multiface Dataset: The public multifaceted dataset [36] contains a collection of audio4D scan pairs captured from 13 subjects, one subject speaking 12 sentences and the others speaking 50 sentences, with 3D facial motion captured at a frame rate of 30FPS. However, both datasets have no variation in head pose. 

A. Qualitative Evaluation 

Although quantitative metrics are essential for evaluating 3D talking face models, qualitative visual analysis is equally indispensable, as it provides a more intuitive demonstration of performance differences among models. To this end, we propose incorporating visual perception as a supplementary evaluation criterion and design a fair comparative experiment: under identical speech input and speaking style conditions, we compare FaceFormer, CodeTalker, and our model (note that PATS does not incorporate speaking style). Experimental results show that compared to CodeTalker and FaceFormer, which exhibit over-smoothed lip movements, our model generates more accurate lip motion trajectories, with amplitudes closely matching real articulations. Partic-ularly when handling emotional multi-syllable expressions (e.g., wide mouth openings for ”make” and ”how,” subtle lip movements for ”decision” and ”him”), our model demonstrates superior adaptability to pronunciation. For further quantita-tive analysis, we introduce a temporal dynamics assessment method: by computing the mean (reflecting motion intensity) and standard deviation (indicating dynamic range) of vertices motion sequences and comparing them with ground-truth, we objectively evaluate the naturalness and expressiveness of facial movements generated by each model. This combined approach of qualitative visualization and quantitative motion analysis provides a more comprehensive evaluation framework for 3D talking face generation.Our method can obtain more accurate facial movements than other methods, and it can be seen from the graph that our method is closest to the reference in terms of the intensity of facial movements.

C. Ablation experiments 

We proposed several key modules including Spatio-Temporal vertex feature Encoding(STE), Residual Graph Non-volutional Networ(RGCN) module and Dual Cross-Attention Mechanis(DCAM). In this section, we conduct ablation studies to evaluate the impact of different components in our proposed MMTalker framework on the quality of the generated 3D speaking faces. 

Spatio-Temporal vertex feature Encoding: STE injects time information into the vertex position features; otherwise, the position features will be replicated T times in the module and cannot generate discriminative features for the vertices of each frame. To evaluate its effectiveness, we implemented a method variant, Remove the STE module and instead use the position encoding without time, table II Three indicators showed significant degradation. This indicates that audio in-formation plays a crucial role in refinement of fine lip move-ments and enhancing audio-visual consistency and rhythmic smoothness. 

Residual Graph Nonvolutional Networ: RGCN fuses local audio with temporal sequence features based on the topological connection relationship of the original mesh,which improves the similarity and difference of each facial area when speaking. We replaced RGCN with MLP. The comparison shows that the values of each evaluation index have all improved. 

Dual Cross-Attention Mechanis: As discussed in Sec. III, global audio features encode the overall attributes of the audio, and local features guide the vertices movement under the global attributes. After removing the DCAM module, we use MLP to directly fuse the global and local features. Compared with MLP( Fl, f g ), we propose that DCAM( Fl, f g ) fuses global and local features, which can generate more accurate facial expressions and avoid the need to replicate fg N times. This leads to an excessively high similarity among each vertices in one frame. V. VI. CONCLUSION This paper introduces MMTalker, a voice-driven 3D face animation method based on multi-modal fusion, which can generate high-fidelity, multi-resolution expressive facial ani-mation. Through the innovative cross-modal coupling design, the method realizes the efficient coordination of speech signal, geometric structure and timing information, and significantly improves the generation quality of complex facial expressions. We designed a local audio feature fusion module to effec-tively enhance lip synchronization accuracy and facial motion consistency. The multi-resolution representation mechanism designed at the same time supports the flexible generation of facial animation with different precision. Quantitative and qualitative experimental analysis shows that MMTalker is superior to the existing methods in terms of quantitative indexes and visual effects, and can generate more accurate and natural facial animation.
